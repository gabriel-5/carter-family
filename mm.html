The first time I ever got fired from a job was when I was nineteen. I thought to myself: “What did I do wrong?”. I had done nothing wrong, except for maybe doing too much right. This is something a lot of entrepreneurs don’t understand the first (...or second) time around. Case closed? No, case open! Sometimes you have to get there the long way round. If you have a patch of infected eczema running down your left arm and it looks like bright orange plastic armour, do I bandage it up? No? That’s right. You don’t need to hide away your truth when it comes to counting costs, or counting chickens (not before they’ve hatched, mind!). There’s a difference between leadership and glider-ship (get it?), and this is what most people don’t get. In my old post at the oil rig, they called me “spiller”. Why? Well, why do you think?
